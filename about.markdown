<div class="timeline-section" style="margin-top: 30px; margin-bottom: 0;">
  <p>My career has moved through three distinct chapters: embedded wireless systems, machine learning modeling and infrastructure, and now accelerated computing. I started in real-time wireless integrating GPS and cellular baseband protocols for Smartphone application at Palm and then Apple, where I also built an internal Knowledge Discovery Framework to bring ML tooling to the wireless engineering group.</p>
  <p>From there I moved up the stack into cloud-based ML platforms, first at Postmates (fraud detection models, ML productionization tooling) and then at C3 AI, where I co-designed the C3 Dataset product — a unified interface for heterogeneous data execution engines. Today I'm focusing on the next layer of that evolution: GPU-accelerated query engines and the AI agents that will drive them.</p>
  <p>I hold an Engineering diploma in Telecommunication and Networks from <a href="https://www.usj.edu.lb/esib/?lang=2">ESIB/USJ</a> in Beirut, a Master's in Electrical Engineering from <a href="https://www.ece.cornell.edu/ece">Cornell University</a>, and have pursued continuing graduate studies in Software Systems, AI/ML, and Entrepreneurship at <a href="https://cgoe.stanford.edu">Stanford University</a> since 2011.</p>
</div>
